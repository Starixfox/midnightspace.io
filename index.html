What we are

An honest early-stage data project

Midnight Space is a solo-founder project in prototype phase. Right now, there is one working demo: a dashboard that maps public Saudi and GCC space-related funding calls, grants, and programs.

We are not a launch company. We do not operate rockets, satellites, or spacecraft. We are building data and analytics tools that help organisations navigate the growing space funding landscape.

Core capabilities

Built around data, not hardware

Everything Midnight Space does is about making space ecosystem data readable, navigable, and actionable.

Opportunity mapping

Funding calls, grants, accelerators, and public programs — consolidated in one searchable database with filters by region, sector, and status.

Program tracking

Monitor open and closed program cycles, recurring calls, and application windows. Know what is active right now and what to anticipate.

Ecosystem dashboards

Visual summaries and charts for leadership teams. Turn raw program data into a clear picture of where activity and investment are concentrated.

Signals & alerts Planned

Notifications when new funding calls open, programs change status, or new sources are added. Coming in a future version.

Who it's for

Built for the people navigating space

Midnight Space is relevant anywhere decisions are shaped by knowledge of funding flows, programs, and ecosystem activity.

🏛️
Governments & public agencies

Track national and international space programs, identify gaps in your funding landscape, and benchmark against peer agencies — all from a dashboard built for decision-makers.

🌐
Economic development offices

Map where public money is flowing in the space ecosystem, identify overlapping or competing programs, and build strategy grounded in real data.

📈
Investors & funds

See where government investment is concentrating before commercial dealflow follows. Use program and grant data as a leading indicator for where to look.

🚀
Ecosystem builders

Accelerators, clusters, and incubators benefit from a clear picture of what programs are active, what calls are open, and where their startups should apply.
